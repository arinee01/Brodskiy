Joseph Brodsky

Joseph Brodsky (1940–1996) was a Russian-American poet and essayist, widely recognized as one of the major literary voices of the 20th century. Born in Leningrad (now St. Petersburg), he was persecuted in the Soviet Union for "social parasitism" and expelled in 1972. He emigrated to the United States, where he taught literature and wrote in both Russian and English. Brodsky received the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1987 and served as U.S. Poet Laureate in 1991.

This project presents ten cultural heritage items related to his life and work, integrating archival, bibliographic, and artistic sources using Linked Open Data principles.
