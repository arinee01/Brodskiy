Joseph Brodsky

Joseph Brodsky (rus. Иосиф Александрович Бродский, 1940–1996) was a Russian and American poet and essayist, Nobel Prize winner. Born in Leningrad (now Saint Petersburg) in the Soviet Union, he was persecuted by Soviet authorities and expelled from the country in 1972, after which he settled in the United States. There, he taught literature and continued writing in both Russian and English.

A special place for Brodsky was always Venice. For over 20 years, he visited the city—usually in winter—and developed a deep personal bond with it. In 1989, he wrote one of his most significant works dedicated to Venice: the lyrical essay “Watermark” (rus. «Набережная неисцелимых», it. “Fondamenta degli incurabili”), composed of brief, reflective chapters exploring his impressions of the city.

Brodsky’s final resting place is also in Venice—on the cemetery island of San Michele.

May 2025 marked the 85th anniversary of Brodsky's birth, and January 2026 will mark 30 years since his passing.

This project presents ten cultural heritage items related to his life and work, integrating archival, bibliographic, and artistic sources using the principles of Linked Open Data.
